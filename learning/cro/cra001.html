With the current trend to move toward a risk-based monitoring approach, CRAs are expected to work closer with the site to provide support and identify needs. This can be a difficult transition for CRAs who have focused primarily on source data review and basic site follow-up.

This course will provide information about what is required for a CRA to be successful in their role, as well as tips and tools to use with the site throughout the clinical trial. Methods to identify potential pitfalls and risk-management strategies will be discussed, and attendees will be led through an interactive case study to demonstrate process improvement techniques, problem-solving and risk identification.

Learning Objectives

  • Discuss the importance of being able to clearly identify site needs.
  • Describe the importance of risk identification and management.
  • List two ways the clinical research associate can impact sites.
